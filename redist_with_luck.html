Welcome

Thank you for taking part in this study on decision-making in economics. The study consists of the following two parts:

Part 1:
Experience Phase You will perform a brief task that requires no previous knowledge. This will help you understand the type of work that others have done in similar situations.
Part 2:
Distribution Phase You will be asked to make a decision about how to allocate earnings between two other participants.

The entire experiment will take approximately 7–10 minutes to complete.

Participant Information & Consent
This study is conducted by Isabella Valencia at the Paris School of Economics, under the supervision of Professor Andrea FM Martinangeli, to study decision-making in economics. Your participation is entirely voluntary and you may withdraw at any time by closing this browser window. Data is collected anonymously under GDPR regulations and will be used for academic purposes only. No identifying information is stored. Part 1:

You are now asked to work on a code recognition task for 5 minutes. We will measure your performance by the number of points you receive.

The scoring works as follows:

  • +1 point for each correct code found.
  • -1 point for each wrong code checked.
  • No penalty for missing a code.

The task consists of 5 rounds. In each round, you have 60 seconds before the matrix submits automatically and you move on to the next round. After completing all 5 rounds, you will be asked to answer a few questions about your experience before moving on to the second part of the study.

This is a simplified example:
If the code you must check off is: 123, you must find and click as many "123" instances as possible in the grid.

Before starting the real task, you will have the opportunity to practice for 15 seconds to familiarize yourself with the interface. This won't affect your score in any way, but we encourage you to try your best in the practice round to get a real feel for the task.

When you have read and understood the instructions press >> to start the short practice session.

Part 2: Distribution Phase

Two previous participants, Worker A and Worker B, were recruited to conduct the exact same task you just completed.

Your Role:

Your task is to decide how to distribute a total payment of $6 between the workers. You can choose to give all the money to one worker, split it equally, or any other division you see fit. Your decision is anonymous and may be the one that determines their final payment. Since we collect decisions from several participants, we will randomly select one person's choice to be implemented. If yours is selected, the workers will be paid exactly as you decide.
